🔬 HEMATOLOGICAL / ONCOLOGICAL
Warfarin (Coumadin) 🏆 Used to prevent blood clots in atrial fibrillation, mechanical heart valves, DVT/PE.
  • Bleeding (GI, intracranial)
  • Skin necrosis
 Monitor INR (2‑3, up to 3.5 for valves); contraindicated in pregnancy. Keep vitamin K intake consistent; use soft toothbrush; report bleeding. Antidote: Vitamin K, FFP. Avoid NSAIDs, many antibiotics.
Heparin 🏆 Used for acute anticoagulation in DVT/PE, MI, and during procedures.
  • Bleeding
  • HIT (thrombocytopenia)
 Monitor aPTT (1.5‑2.5× control), platelets; avoid with active bleeding. Report bruising, dark stools, unusual bleeding. Antidote: Protamine sulfate. Avoid with antiplatelets.
Enoxaparin (Lovenox) 🏆 Used to prevent and treat DVT/PE, especially after surgery.
  • Bleeding
  • Epidural hematoma risk with spinal anesthesia
 Monitor platelets; anti‑Xa rarely; avoid with active bleeding. Subcutaneous; do not rub site; report unusual bruising. Antidote: Protamine (partial). Avoid NSAIDs.
Clopidogrel (Plavix) 🏆 Used to prevent stent thrombosis, stroke, and heart attack.
  • Bleeding
  • TTP (rare)
 Hold 5‑7d before surgery; monitor for bruising. Report unusual bleeding, black tarry stools. Avoid omeprazole (↓ efficacy); avoid NSAIDs.
Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) Used to prevent clots in atrial fibrillation and treat DVT/PE.
  • Bleeding
 CrCl <15 avoid; no routine lab monitoring. Take with evening meal (food increases absorption). Antidote: Andexanet alfa. Avoid NSAIDs.
Apixaban (Eliquis) Used to prevent stroke in AF and treat DVT/PE.
  • Bleeding
 CrCl <25 avoid; no routine monitoring. Take as prescribed; missed dose can be taken up to 6h before next. Antidote: Andexanet alfa. Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors increase levels.
Dabigatran (Pradaxa) Used to prevent stroke in AF and treat DVT/PE.
  • Bleeding
  • Indigestion
 CrCl <30 avoid; capsules must not be opened. Swallow whole; report indigestion, bleeding. Antidote: Idarucizumab (Praxbind). P‑gp inhibitors increase levels.
Fondaparinux (Arixtra) Used to prevent DVT after orthopedic surgery.
  • Bleeding
 CrCl <30 avoid; monitor platelets. Subcutaneous; do not mix with other injections. No specific antidote; avoid with neuraxial anesthesia.
Filgrastim (Neupogen) Used to boost neutrophil count after chemotherapy or in neutropenia.
  • Bone pain
  • Splenic rupture (rare)
 Monitor CBC with differential; avoid in sickle cell disease. Report severe left upper quadrant pain, fever. No major interactions.
Epoetin alfa (Procrit) Used to treat anemia in chronic kidney disease or from chemotherapy.
  • High blood pressure
  • Blood clots
 Monitor Hgb (target ≤11 g/dL), BP, iron stores; avoid with uncontrolled HTN. Report signs of clots (leg pain, chest pain); take iron as directed. No specific antidote.
Methotrexate Used for rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis, and certain cancers.
  • Bone marrow suppression
  • Liver damage
  • Lung fibrosis
 Monitor LFTs, Cr, CBC; contraindicated in pregnancy, alcoholism. Avoid alcohol; take folic acid; report dry cough, bleeding. Antidote: Leucovorin (for high doses). NSAIDs ↑ toxicity.
Tamoxifen Used to treat and prevent hormone‑positive breast cancer.
  • Hot flashes
  • Blood clots
  • Endometrial cancer
 Monitor for vaginal bleeding; avoid in pregnancy. Report abnormal vaginal bleeding, leg swelling/pain. Strong CYP2D6 inhibitors (paroxetine) ↓ efficacy.
❤️ CARDIOVASCULAR
Furosemide (Lasix) 🏆 Used to remove excess fluid in heart failure, kidney disease, and hypertension.
  • Low potassium
  • Hearing damage (rapid IV)
  • Dehydration
 Monitor K+, Mg, Cr, BP; contraindicated in anuria. Weigh daily, eat potassium‑rich foods, change position slowly. Digoxin toxicity (if low K). Avoid ototoxic drugs.
Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) 🏆 Used to lower blood pressure and reduce mild edema.
  • Low potassium
  • High blood sugar
  • High uric acid
  • Sun sensitivity
 Monitor K+, glucose, uric acid; caution with sulfa allergy. Take in morning; avoid sunlight; report muscle weakness. Lithium ↑ levels; NSAIDs ↓ effect.
Spironolactone 🏆 Used for heart failure, high blood pressure, and excess aldosterone.
  • High potassium
  • Breast tenderness/enlargement
 Monitor K+, Cr; avoid in anuria, high K. Avoid salt substitutes, high‑K foods; report breast tenderness. ACEi/ARBs ↑ hyperkalemia risk.
Bumetanide (Bumex) Used for fluid overload in heart failure or kidney disease.
  • Low potassium
  • Hearing damage
 Monitor K+, Cr, BP. Same as furosemide. Same as furosemide.
Chlorthalidone Used to lower blood pressure (longer‑acting thiazide).
  • Low potassium
  • High blood sugar
 Monitor K+, glucose. Take in morning; photosensitivity precautions. Similar to HCTZ.
Metoprolol (Lopressor) 🏆 Used for high blood pressure, angina, heart failure, and after heart attack.

  • Fatigue

 Hold for HR <60, SBP <90; avoid in asthma (relative). Do not stop abruptly; monitor HR/BP; report dizziness. Verapamil/diltiazem ↑ bradycardia risk.
Carvedilol (Coreg) Used for heart failure and high blood pressure (nonselective beta‑blocker).

 Monitor HR, BP, weight (fluid retention). Take with food; rise slowly. Same as metoprolol.
Propranolol (Inderal) Used for high blood pressure, migraine, essential tremor, and stage fright.

 Contraindicated in asthma, heart block; monitor HR. Same; report wheezing. Same as metoprolol.
Atenolol (Tenormin) Used for high blood pressure and angina (cardioselective).

  • Cold hands/feet
 Monitor HR, BP. Do not stop abruptly. Less CNS effects, otherwise similar.
Lisinopril (Prinivil) 🏆 Used for high blood pressure, heart failure, and protecting kidneys in diabetes.

  • High potassium
  • Swelling of lips/face (angioedema)
 Monitor K+, Cr; avoid in pregnancy, hx of angioedema. Report swelling of lips/face, persistent cough. ARBs alternative. Avoid K+ supplements, NSAIDs.
Enalapril (Vasotec) Same as lisinopril – ACE inhibitor for HTN, HF. Same. Same. Same. Same.
Captopril (Capoten) ACE inhibitor; used for HTN, HF (short‑acting). Same + rash, taste disturbance. Same. Take 1h before meals. Same.

Amlodipine (Norvasc) 🏆 Used for high blood pressure and chest pain (angina).

  • Headache

Nifedipine (Procardia) Used for HTN and angina (short‑acting mostly avoided). Same + reflex fast heart rate. Monitor BP, HR. Same. Same.
Diltiazem (Cardizem) 🏆 Used for fast heart rate (AFib), angina, and HTN.

  • Constipation
  • Heart block

Verapamil (Calan) Used for AFib, angina, HTN – more constipating. Same + more constipation. Same. Same. Same.
Digoxin (Lanoxin) 🏆 Used for heart failure and to control ventricular rate in AFib.

  • Vision changes (yellow halos)
  • Irregular heartbeat
 Monitor level (0.5‑2 ng/mL), K+, Mg, Cr; hold HR <60. Take pulse daily; report vision changes, N/V. Low K ↑ toxicity. Antidote: Digibind.
Nitroglycerin (NTG) 🏆 Used to relieve acute chest pain (angina).
  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Flushing
 Contraindicated with PDE‑5 inhibitors (sildenafil). Sit/lie down; call 911 if no relief after 1 tab; protect from light. Severe hypotension with PDE‑5 inhibitors.
Isosorbide mononitrate Used to prevent angina (long‑acting nitrate). Headache, hypotension. Same as NTG. Take regularly; tolerance possible. Same.
Atorvastatin (Lipitor) 🏆 Used to lower cholesterol and reduce heart attack/stroke risk.
  • Muscle aches
  • Liver damage

Simvastatin (Zocor) Same as atorvastatin – cholesterol lowering. Same. Same. Take in evening; avoid grapefruit. Same; stronger interactions with amiodarone, verapamil.
Rosuvastatin (Crestor) Same – potent statin for LDL lowering. Same. Same. Take any time; report muscle pain. Less CYP interaction, but still monitor.
Clonidine (Catapres) 🏆 Used for high blood pressure (second‑line).
  • Dry mouth
  • Drowsiness
  • Rebound high BP if stopped suddenly
 Monitor BP; do not stop abruptly. Do not skip doses; taper when discontinuing. Beta‑blockers ↑ rebound risk.
Alteplase (tPA) 🏆 Used to dissolve clots in ischemic stroke, massive PE, and heart attack.
  • Bleeding (especially in the brain)
 Strict contraindications: recent stroke/surgery, bleeding risk. N/A (hospital use only). Avoid antiplatelets/anticoagulants during infusion.
Adenosine (Adenocard) Used to stop certain fast heart rhythms (PSVT).
  • Brief pause in heartbeat
  • Flushing
  • Chest pressure
 ECG monitoring; caution in asthma. Explain transient symptoms. Caffeine/theophylline block effect.
Amiodarone (Cordarone) Used for life‑threatening ventricular arrhythmias and AFib.
  • Lung scarring
  • Thyroid problems
  • Corneal deposits
  • Blue‑gray skin
 Monitor LFTs, TFTs, chest x‑ray, eye exams; avoid in severe bradycardia. Report shortness of breath, vision changes, skin discoloration. Increases levels of warfarin, digoxin, statins.
Milrinone (Primacor) Used short‑term for severe heart failure (increases heart contraction).
  • Low blood pressure
  • Arrhythmias
  • Low platelets
 Monitor BP, HR, platelets. N/A (hospital use). May potentiate vasodilators.

Beclomethasone (QVAR) Inhaled steroid for asthma control. Same as fluticasone. Same. Same. Same.
Methylprednisolone (Solu‑Medrol) 🏆 Used for acute asthma, allergic reactions (IV/PO steroid).

  • Mood changes

Montelukast (Singulair) Used for asthma and allergies (leukotriene blocker).

Docusate sodium (Colace) Used to soften stools (laxative) – prevents straining.
